Storyline

With Holland being a coastal country and with one-sixth of its land mass covered by water, the Dutch are adept at moving via the water. Although many of the conveyances are for work purposes, there are just as many which are used more for recreational pursuits. In combination with sufficient winds due to the flat land, competitive and pleasure sailing are major activities, especially on summer weekends, with the English word yacht derived from the Dutch language. Activities during a week of a race near Leiden and another race for larger sailboats off the coast of Northern Holland are shown, with Dutch of all ages participating in the fun in one form or another. Would You Say That One More Time?
1 June 2017 | by boblipton – See all my reviews

RKO's once-mighty short subject division was down to the occasional Disney short by this point, and its Sportscope one-reelers. The latter wouldn't last much longer; only four more would be released over the next year.

This one is about sailboat racing in Holland, from toy boats through two-riggers. There are few more beautiful things in motion that a small boat, its sails filled with wind, passing by, and this one shows you plenty of those.

Harry Wismer's narration is not as good as the visuals. He is one of those voice-over workers who sounds like he is shouting something very important at you, even as he tells the same joke about Dutch boys sticking their fingers into dikes at least twice. I stopped listening.
